## The Question

When a transaction involves a recipient located outside India and the place of supply is determined to be outside India, is it still a "supply" within the meaning of the GST law? And if so, is it taxable?

This question has generated significant litigation and conflicting advance rulings since the introduction of GST in 2017. It sits at the intersection of the territorial scope of the GST statute, the place of supply provisions, and the zero-rating framework for exports.

---

## The Statutory Framework

### Taxability Under the CGST Act

Section 9 of the CGST Act, 2017 levies Central GST on the **intra-State supply** of goods or services. Section 5 of the IGST Act, 2017 levies Integrated GST on the **inter-State supply** of goods or services. Section 7 of the IGST Act provides that a supply of services is treated as an inter-State supply where the location of the supplier and the place of supply are in different States or where either is outside India.

The critical provision is Section 13 of the IGST Act, which determines the **place of supply of services** where either the supplier or the recipient is located outside India.

### Place of Supply Outside India

Where the place of supply determined under Section 13 falls **outside India**, a literal reading suggests that the supply falls outside the scope of IGST. The IGST Act is stated to extend to "the whole of India" — and if the place of supply is outside India, there is an argument that the supply does not occur within the taxable territory.

---

## The Competing Interpretations

### View 1: Place of Supply Outside India = Not Taxable

Under this view, the place of supply provisions are not merely administrative — they define the territorial scope of the levy. If the place of supply is outside India, the supply does not fall within the charging section of the IGST Act, and no tax is leviable.

This view finds support in:

- The constitutional basis of GST, which limits the levy to supplies within Indian territory
- The structure of the IGST Act, which uses place of supply to determine whether a supply is inter-State
- The analogy with customs law, where taxability is tied to the point of importation into India

### View 2: Supply Remains Taxable; Only Zero-Rating Applies

Under this competing view — advanced by Revenue in several proceedings — the place of supply provision does not determine taxability but only determines the **rate** and **entitlement to input tax credit**. The supply remains taxable, but at zero percent if it qualifies as an export of services under Section 2(6) of the IGST Act.

> "The question of whether a supply with place of supply outside India is outside the GST net entirely, or is merely zero-rated, has significant practical consequences for ITC entitlement, refund eligibility, and compliance obligations."

---

## Export of Services: The Safe Harbour

Section 2(6) of the IGST Act defines **export of services** as a supply of services where:

1. The supplier is located in India
2. The recipient is located outside India
3. The place of supply is outside India
4. The payment is received in convertible foreign exchange (or in Indian Rupees where permitted by RBI)
5. The supplier and recipient are not merely establishments of the same person

Where all five conditions are met, the supply is zero-rated under Section 16 of the IGST Act. The supplier can either:

- Supply under a Letter of Undertaking (LUT) without payment of tax and claim refund of accumulated ITC, or
- Pay IGST and claim a refund

### The Consequence of Non-Qualification

Where a supply does not meet all five conditions for export of services — for instance, because payment is not received in foreign exchange, or because the supplier and recipient are related establishments — the supply may:

- Be taxable at the standard rate if the place of supply is within India
- Fall outside the GST net if the place of supply is outside India (under View 1 above)
- Be taxable but not zero-rated (under View 2 above)

---

## Key Scenarios

| Scenario | Place of Supply | GST Treatment |
|----------|----------------|---------------|
| IT services to foreign company, POS outside India, payment in forex | Outside India | Zero-rated export (if conditions met) |
| Intermediary services, recipient outside India | Location of supplier (India) | Taxable at standard rate |
| Services to branch of Indian company outside India | Outside India | Disputed — establishment of same person |
| Online information services to unregistered foreign recipient | Location of recipient (outside India) | Outside scope or zero-rated |

---

## Advance Ruling Conflicts

Several State Advance Ruling Authorities have taken conflicting positions:

- Some authorities have held that where the place of supply is outside India, the supply is entirely outside the scope of GST and no tax is leviable — nor is any refund of ITC available
- Others have held that the supply remains taxable under IGST at zero percent, entitling the supplier to ITC refund

The absence of a binding national ruling on this issue means that the position varies by State and fact pattern.

---

## Conclusion

The question of whether a supply with a place of supply outside India falls within the taxable territory of GST remains unresolved at the appellate level. Businesses providing cross-border services should:

1. Map each service line against the place of supply rules under Section 13
2. Assess qualification for export of services under Section 2(6)
3. Structure foreign exchange receipt and related-party arrangements to preserve zero-rating eligibility
4. Obtain advance rulings where the position is genuinely uncertain
